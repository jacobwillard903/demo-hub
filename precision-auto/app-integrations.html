Integrations

The AI works inside the tools the shop already runs. Nothing gets replaced.

TekmetricConnected
Shop management

The system of record. The AI reads ROs, bay status, and DVIs, and writes appointments and notes straight back. Every booking on the phone page landed here.

QuickBooks OnlineConnected
Accounting

Feeds the Financials page. Transactions categorized to the shop chart of accounts, payouts matched to deposits, fleet invoices nudged on schedule.

PartsTechConnected
Parts ordering

Live parts pricing and availability while quoting. Backorders push a customer text automatically, and statements reconcile against the books.

CARFAXConnected
Vehicle history and VIN decode

A plate or VIN becomes year, make, engine, and service history at write-up. No more asking the customer what motor is in it.

NHTSA RecallsConnected
Open recall lookup

Every VIN checked for open recalls at check-in. The advisor mentions it before the dealer does, which builds trust and the occasional extra visit.

TwilioConnected
Voice and SMS

The shop's phone line and text number. Powers the receptionist, the estimate chaser, the status responder, and the recovery texts.

StripeConnected
Payments

Pay by link in the ready text, deposits for big jobs, and settlements that match themselves to the bank in QuickBooks.

Google Business ProfileConnected
Reviews and local search

Review requests an hour after pickup, unhappy customers routed private to Gary first. 4.9 stars and climbing.

Gmail and Google CalendarConnected
Email and scheduling

Fleet invoices and estimate PDFs go out from the shop's own address. Loaner handoffs and vendor visits land on the shared calendar.

SunbitAvailable
Repair financing

Pay over time on big tickets. A $2,150 transmission service is an easier yes at $95 a month, offered right in the estimate text.

Mitchell 1 ProDemandAvailable
Labor times and repair info

Book time and OEM procedures pulled into estimates automatically, so quoted hours match what the job actually takes.

Your next toolOpen
Anything with an API

Tire suppliers, fleet cards, a second location. If the shop adds a tool, the AI learns to use it. The stack bends to the shop, not the other way around.
